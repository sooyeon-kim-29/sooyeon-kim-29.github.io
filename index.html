I am a PhD candidate in Environmental Health Sciences at George Washington University, where I study how air pollution affect population health with Dr. Susan Anenberg. I've completed my undergraduate and Master's at Korea University, majoring in Environmental Health Sciences and Epidemiology.

My research aims to generate evidence that supports equitable and data-driven environmental health policy. To provide the evidence, I integrate environmental exposure models, geospatial analytics, and epidemiological methods to understand how environmental risks, especially air pollution, are distributed across time, space, and different communities.

Research interests:

  • Spatial analysis & large-scale geospatial data science
  • Air pollution epidemiology
  • Health burden estimation (GBD frameworks)
  • Health impacts of climate change
  • Environmental justice and distributive inequality
